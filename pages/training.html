Prompt Engineering for Strategic Sourcing

A comprehensive training session to equip the sourcing team with fundamental skills and techniques of prompt engineering, enabling them to use AI tools to generate specific, accurate, and high-quality outputs for their daily tasks.

Part 1: The Foundation

Why This is the Most Important Skill

  • Core Principle: AI is a tool, and the prompt is how you control it.
  • Analogy: Learning to prompt is like learning to use the advanced functions on a calculator instead of just addition and subtraction. It unlocks the tool's true power.
  • Golden Rule: "Garbage In, Garbage Out." A vague prompt leads to a generic, often useless, result. A specific, well-structured prompt leads to a precise, valuable result.

The Goal of a Good Prompt

  • It's not a search query; it's a detailed instruction set.
  • It should eliminate ambiguity and clearly define a successful outcome.

Part 2: The Anatomy of a Powerful Prompt

The C-R-A-F-T Framework

A memorable acronym for building great prompts that deliver precise, valuable results.

C - Context

What it is: Set the scene for the AI. Tell it what information it needs to consider.

Why it matters: Context narrows the AI's focus, leading to more relevant answers.

Example: "I am a sourcing manager at a large public university. We are currently evaluating suppliers for a new campus-wide landscaping contract."

R - Role (or Persona)

What it is: Assign a specific role to the AI.

Why it matters: This influences the tone, style, and expertise of the response.

Example: "Act as an expert in contract law with a specialty in vendor agreements." or "Act as a skeptical financial analyst."

A - Action (The Task)

What it is: The specific verb or command you want the AI to perform.

Why it matters: This is the core instruction. Use strong action verbs.

Examples: "Analyze," "Compare," "Draft," "Summarize," "Brainstorm," "Rewrite," "Translate."

F - Format

What it is: Define the structure of the output.

Why it matters: It makes the information immediately usable without extra work.

Examples: "...in a markdown table," "...as a bulleted list," "...as a professional email," "...in a JSON format," "...as a series of questions."

T - Tone

What it is: Specify the desired writing style.

Why it matters: Ensures the output is appropriate for the intended audience.

Examples: "...in a formal and professional tone," "...in a simple, easy-to-understand manner," "...in a persuasive and confident tone."

Part 3: Building Prompts with C-R-A-F-T

Exercise 1: Transforming a Vague Request

Simple Prompt:

"Tell me about supplier risks."

Tailored Prompt using C-R-A-F-T:

(C)ontext: "We are considering a five-year contract with a new, privately-owned IT hardware supplier based overseas."

(R)ole: "Act as a supply chain risk assessment expert."

(A)ction: "Brainstorm a list of potential risks associated with this partnership."

(F)ormat: "Organize the risks into three categories: financial, geopolitical, and operational. Present this as a bulleted list under each category heading."

(T)one: "Use a clear, concise, and objective tone."

Exercise 2: The Iterative Process

Concept: Your first prompt is rarely your last. Show how to refine a prompt based on the AI's output.

Step 1: Run the good prompt from Exercise 1.
Step 2: Analyze the output. Maybe it's too general.
Step 3: Refine the prompt: "Excellent, now for each of the operational risks you identified, suggest one potential mitigation strategy that a university procurement team could implement."

Part 4: Advanced Techniques

Few-Shot Prompting

Provide examples within your prompt to guide the AI's output format and style.

Example: "Rewrite the following sentences to be more concise. Example: 'At this point in time, we are of the opinion that...' should become 'We believe...'. Now, rewrite this: 'It is incumbent upon the vendor to ensure compliance...'"

Chain-of-Thought

Ask the AI to explain its reasoning step-by-step for more accurate results on complex problems.

Example: "...Analyze the attached proposal and determine the best value. Explain your reasoning step-by-step, considering initial cost, long-term support, and implementation time."

Negative Constraints

Tell the AI what NOT to do to avoid unwanted outputs.

Example: "...Draft an email to the supplier. Do not mention specific budget numbers and avoid making any promises about contract length."

Part 5: Conclusion & Q&A

Key Takeaways

  • Master the C-R-A-F-T Framework: Context, Role, Action, Format, Tone
  • Practice Makes Perfect: Prompting is a skill that improves with practice
  • Be a Co-Pilot: Guide the AI to produce the work you need
  • Iterate and Refine: Your first prompt is rarely your last

Next Steps

  • Practice the C-R-A-F-T framework with your daily tasks
  • Experiment with different techniques (few-shot, chain-of-thought, negative constraints)
  • Share successful prompts with your team
